BlogIntroduction to Cognitive Behavioural Thinking (CBT)28 Dec, 2022
Photo by Ryan Gagnon on Unsplash

Depression can be a challenging and overwhelming experience, leaving individuals feeling stuck and helpless in their negative thoughts and emotions. However, there is hope and effective treatment options available, such as Cognitive Behaviour Therapy (CBT).

CBT is a form of psychotherapy that focuses on identifying and changing negative thoughts and behaviours in order to improve mood and overall well-being. It was developed by Aaron Beck in the 1960s and has since become a widely used and evidence-based treatment for a variety of mental health conditions, including depression, anxiety disorders, eating disorders, substance abuse disorders, and personality disorders.

One of the key techniques used in CBT is "homework" assignments, which involve the client engaging in specific activities or tasks outside of therapy sessions. These activities can include keeping a record of moods and thoughts, or engaging in enjoyable activities for a specific amount of time each day. The aim of these assignments is to help the client develop new skills and habits to cope with stress and negative emotions more effectively.

CBT can also help individuals identify and challenge cognitive distortions, or "thinking errors" that can contribute to negative thoughts and emotions. Examples of cognitive distortions include black-and-white thinking, jumping to conclusions, personalization, should statements, mental filter, overgeneralization, magnification and minimization, fortune-telling, comparison, catastrophizing, labeling, and disqualifying the positive.

CBT typically lasts for several weeks or months and is designed to teach clients skills that they can use throughout their lives to manage their mental health. An example of the effectiveness of CBT is the case of Sarah, a 19-year-old college student struggling with depression. Through CBT, Sarah was able to identify and challenge negative beliefs and focus on her strengths and passions, leading to an improvement in her depression and a sense of purpose and meaning in her life.

Overall, CBT is a highly effective treatment for a wide range of mental health conditions and can be a powerful tool in helping individuals overcome depression and improve their overall well-being. So, it is a great way to overcome depression and lead a happy life.
