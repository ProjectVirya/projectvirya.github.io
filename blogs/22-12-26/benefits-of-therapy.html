BlogBenefits of therapy26 Dec, 2022
Photo by Ashkan Forouzani on Unsplash

People are afraid because group therapy increases their vulnerability - more people, more exposure. But if we can get past that feeling and try it on our own, it can be very beneficial. In group therapy, you have the opportunity to lean into feeling accepted by others while also challenging yourself to make your needs known. This can include pushing yourself out of your comfort zone and speaking in front of a group, as well as giving yourself validation that you were heard. According to one 2019 study, group therapy can be especially beneficial when dealing with social anxiety and life transitions.

  1. One of the most important advantages is discovering that you are not alone in your suffering. The shared understanding of a difficult experience fosters trust and reduces the likelihood of any judgment.
  2. People with different personalities and experiences look at issues and problems in different ways. You can incorporate different strategies to address your own issues and problems by observing how others handle these issues and problems.
  3. People with different personalities and experiences tend to approach issues and problems in different ways. You can incorporate different strategies to address your own issues and problems by observing how others handle these issues and problems.
  4. With differences that exist It can also teach you how to properly communicate problems or issues and how to accept criticism. When you're in a safe environment with group members you relate to and who can relate to you about similar issues, you can develop a sense of self-awareness.
  5. When connecting with other group members, it can be beneficial to express your own feelings as they occur. This can boost your confidence and motivate you to work toward positive change.
  6. This is a place where you can think and feel freely. You don't have to change your communication style depending on who you're talking to.
  7. Learning about yourself and others can help you overcome fears, learn how to set healthy relationship boundaries, practice and improve your communication skills, and much more.
